# Phase 1: Business Understanding | HR Attrition Analysis

---

## 1. Business Problem

Employee attrition affects productivity, recruitment costs, workforce stability, and overall business performance. HR lacks a centralized analytical solution to identify the key factors driving employee turnover and support proactive retention strategies.

---

## 2. Business Background

The organization maintains employee records containing demographic, employment, compensation, and performance information.

**Dataset Overview**
- Total Records: **10,000**
- Total Columns: **17**

**Key Attributes**
- Employee ID
- Age
- Gender
- Department
- Job Role
- Education Level
- Marital Status
- Years at Company
- Monthly Salary
- Overtime Status
- Work-Life Balance
- Job Satisfaction
- Performance Rating
- Training Hours Last Year
- Promotion in Last 3 Years
- Remote Work Status
- Attrition Status

The analysis aims to identify employee turnover patterns and support HR decision-making.

---

## 3. Business Objectives

- Identify key factors influencing attrition.
- Analyze attrition by department and job role.
- Evaluate salary and experience trends.
- Measure the impact of overtime, work-life balance, and job satisfaction.
- Identify high-risk employee groups.
- Support HR retention planning with data-driven insights.

---

## 4. Business Questions

- What is the overall attrition rate?
- Which departments have the highest attrition?
- Which job roles experience the most turnover?
- How do overtime and work-life balance affect attrition?
- Does monthly salary influence attrition?
- How do years at the company impact attrition?
- Does job satisfaction affect employee turnover?
- What is the relationship between promotions and attrition?
- Does remote work influence retention?
- Which employee groups require retention focus?

---

## 5. Stakeholders

- Human Resources (HR)
- HR Managers
- Talent Acquisition Team
- Department Managers
- Senior Management
- Business Leadership

---

## 6. Business Impact

- Reduce employee turnover.
- Improve workforce planning.
- Lower recruitment costs.
- Increase employee engagement.
- Improve productivity.
- Support data-driven HR decisions.

---

## 7. Success Criteria

- Identify key attrition drivers.
- Deliver interactive dashboards.
- Highlight high-risk employee groups.
- Provide actionable insights.
- Support informed retention decisions.

---

## 8. Assumptions

- Employee records are accurate.
- Attrition status is correctly recorded.
- Compensation and performance data are reliable.
- Historical data represents workforce trends.
- Duplicate records have been removed.

---

## 9. Constraints

- Analysis is based only on available historical data.
- External factors are not included.
- Attrition reasons are unavailable.
- Findings represent historical trends only.
- Results depend on data quality.

---

## 10. Scope

The project focuses on:

- Department-wise attrition analysis.
- Job role analysis.
- Demographic analysis.
- Salary and experience analysis.
- Work-life balance and overtime analysis.
- High-risk employee identification.
- Power BI dashboard development.

---

## 11. KPIs

1. Overall Employee Attrition Rate (%)
2. Total Employees
3. Total Employees Left
4. Department-wise Attrition Rate
5. Job Role-wise Attrition Rate
6. Average Monthly Salary (Stayed vs Left)

---

## 12. Expected Deliverables

- Clean employee dataset
- SQL analysis
- Python EDA
- Power BI dashboard
- KPI summary
- Business recommendations

---

## 13. Risks

- Missing or inaccurate records.
- Limited information on resignation reasons.
- Historical data may not predict future trends.
- Data quality may affect insights.
- Additional HR context may be required.

---

## 14. Business Recommendations

- Focus retention efforts on high-attrition departments and roles.
- Monitor overtime and workload.
- Improve work-life balance initiatives.
- Review compensation strategies.
- Enhance training and career growth opportunities.
- Track attrition regularly through HR dashboards.
